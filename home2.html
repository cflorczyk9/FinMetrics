Equity Metrics


Ratio Value What is this? Formula

CAGR

 {{CAGR_stock}} The compound annual growth rate is the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period of time longer than one year.

Volatility

 {{volatility}} Volatility is a statistical measure of the dispersion of returns for a given security or market index.

Beta

 {{correlation}} Beta is a measure of the systematic risk of a security compared to a benchmark.

Alpha

 {{alpha}} The excess return of an investment relative to the return of a benchmark index.

Sharpe Ratio

 {{sharpe}} The Sharpe Ratio describes how much excess return you receive for the extra volatility you endure for holding a riskier asset.

Sortino Ratio

 {{sortino}} The Sortino ratio is a variation of the Sharpe ratio that uses the asset's negative standard deviation (downside deviation) instead of the total standard deviation of returns.

Calmar Ratio

 {{calmar}} The Calmar Ratio is used in measuring the performance of an investment by comparing the annual compounded rate of return and the maximum drawdown risk of the investment.

Maximum Drawdown

 {{maximum_drawdown}} Maximum Drawdown is the maximum observed loss from a peak to a trough of a portfolio, before a new peak is attained.

Value At Risk (Var) - 90%

 {{VaR_90}} VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (90% Confidence Level).

Value At Risk (Var) - 95%

 {{VaR_95}} VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (95% Confidence Level).

Value At Risk (Var) - 99%

 {{VaR_99}} VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (99% Confidence Level).
